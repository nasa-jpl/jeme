# LES - Large Eddy Simulation

## Overview

Large Eddy Simulation (LES) is a high-resolution numerical technique for modeling turbulent flows in the atmosphere. LES explicitly resolves large-scale turbulent eddies while parameterizing smaller-scale motions, providing detailed insights into atmospheric boundary layer dynamics, cloud processes, and turbulent transport that are crucial for understanding weather and climate.

## Background

LES emerged from the need to better understand turbulent processes that are poorly represented in traditional atmospheric models. By explicitly simulating the energy-containing eddies responsible for most turbulent transport while modeling only the smallest scales, LES bridges the gap between direct numerical simulation and parameterized approaches, offering unprecedented detail in atmospheric process studies.

## Key Features

- **Explicit Turbulence Resolution**: Direct simulation of large-scale turbulent eddies
- **High Spatial Resolution**: Typical grid spacing of 1-100 meters
- **Subgrid-Scale Modeling**: Parameterization of unresolved small-scale turbulence
- **Cloud-Resolving Capability**: Explicit representation of individual clouds
- **Boundary Layer Studies**: Detailed simulation of PBL structure and evolution
- **Process Understanding**: Insight into fundamental atmospheric processes

## Scientific Applications

- **Boundary Layer Research**: Understanding PBL structure, evolution, and entrainment
- **Cloud Dynamics**: Study of cumulus, stratocumulus, and mixed-phase clouds
- **Turbulence Studies**: Investigation of turbulent transport mechanisms
- **Parameterization Development**: Testing and improving model parameterizations
- **Urban Meteorology**: High-resolution modeling of urban boundary layers
- **Air-Sea Interaction**: Study of marine atmospheric boundary layer processes
- **Wind Energy**: Detailed simulation of wind farm flows and turbine interactions

## Technical Details

**Model Type**: Large Eddy Simulation
**Domain**: Local to Regional Scale (typically < 100 km)
**Spatial Resolution**: 1-100 meters
**Temporal Resolution**: Seconds to Minutes
**Programming Language**: Fortran, C++, Python
**Computational Requirements**: High-performance computing systems
