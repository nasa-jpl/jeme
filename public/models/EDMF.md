# EDMF - Eddy Diffusivity Mass Flux Scheme

## Overview

The Eddy Diffusivity Mass Flux (EDMF) scheme is a parameterization approach that represents turbulent mixing and convective transport processes in atmospheric models. EDMF combines the traditional eddy diffusivity approach for small-scale turbulence with a mass flux representation for organized convective structures, providing a unified framework for boundary layer and shallow convection modeling.

## Background

EDMF was developed to overcome limitations of traditional turbulence parameterizations that struggled to represent the full range of turbulent motions in the planetary boundary layer. By combining eddy diffusivity for background turbulence with explicit representation of coherent updrafts and downdrafts, EDMF provides a more physically realistic treatment of boundary layer processes and their transition to shallow convection.

## Key Features

- **Unified Framework**: Combines eddy diffusivity and mass flux approaches
- **Convective Structures**: Explicit representation of updrafts and downdrafts
- **Boundary Layer Dynamics**: Improved representation of PBL mixing processes
- **Shallow Convection**: Seamless transition from dry to moist convection
- **Vertical Transport**: Enhanced treatment of heat, moisture, and momentum transport
- **Cloud Processes**: Integration with cloud microphysics schemes

## Scientific Applications

- **Weather Prediction**: Improved forecast skill for boundary layer weather
- **Climate Modeling**: Better representation of climate sensitivity and feedbacks
- **Air Quality**: Enhanced simulation of pollutant dispersion
- **Cloud Formation**: Improved shallow cloud and fog prediction
- **Surface-Atmosphere Coupling**: Better representation of land-atmosphere interactions
- **Marine Boundary Layer**: Enhanced modeling of oceanic boundary layers

## Technical Details

**Domain**: Regional to Global Scale
**Spatial Resolution**: Variable (1-100 km typical)
**Temporal Resolution**: Minutes to Hours
**Programming Language**: Fortran, Python
**Applications**: Weather and Climate Models
