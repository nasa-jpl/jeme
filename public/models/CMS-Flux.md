# CMS-Flux - Carbon Monitoring System Flux

## Overview

NASA Carbon Monitoring System Flux (CMS-Flux) is a global carbon cycle data assimilation system that quantifies the spatial and process drivers of atmospheric CO2. The system integrates atmospheric CO2 observations with state-of-the-art atmospheric transport and terrestrial biosphere models to provide high-resolution estimates of carbon sources and sinks.

## Background

CMS-Flux was developed as part of NASA's Carbon Monitoring System to support carbon cycle science and inform climate policy. The system uses advanced inverse modeling techniques to estimate carbon fluxes from the atmosphere back to the surface, providing critical insights into where and when carbon is being absorbed or released by terrestrial and oceanic systems.

## Key Features

- **Atmospheric Inversion**: Top-down constraints on surface carbon fluxes from atmospheric CO2
- **High-Resolution Estimates**: Fine-scale spatial patterns of carbon sources and sinks
- **Multi-Model Ensemble**: Combines multiple models to reduce structural uncertainty
- **Process Attribution**: Identifies contributions from different carbon flux processes
- **Satellite Integration**: Incorporates OCO-2, GOSAT, and other satellite observations
- **Uncertainty Quantification**: Rigorous assessment of flux estimate uncertainties

## Scientific Applications

- **Carbon Budget Analysis**: Regional to global carbon budget assessments
- **Climate Policy Support**: Verification of greenhouse gas inventories
- **Ecosystem Monitoring**: Tracking terrestrial carbon uptake and release
- **Agricultural Emissions**: Quantification of agricultural carbon fluxes
- **Fire Emissions**: Assessment of biomass burning carbon release
- **Fossil Fuel Tracking**: Urban and industrial CO2 emission estimation
- **Climate Model Validation**: Benchmarking Earth system model predictions

## Technical Details

**Model Type**: Atmospheric CO2 Inversion System
**Domain**: Global with Regional Focus Capability
**Spatial Resolution**: 1° x 1° to 0.5° x 0.5°
**Temporal Resolution**: Monthly to Daily
**Programming Language**: Python, Fortran, IDL
**Key Components**: GEOS-Chem, TM5, CarbonTracker
